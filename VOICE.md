# VOICE.md - Kan's Writing Voice

Reference this when co-writing blogs or drafting content.

## Tone
- **Direct and confident.** States opinions as facts, then backs them up. No hedging ("I think maybe..."), no corporate speak.
- **Conversational but sharp.** Reads like a smart friend explaining something at dinner, not a professor lecturing.
- **Dry humor.** Wit comes from unexpected observations, not jokes. ("I am 30 years old" / "attack him with kitchen utensils")

## Structure
- **Opens with a bold claim or hook.** First line grabs you. ("Your company is most likely losing millions of dollars due to remote work." / "As societies mature, the total amount of acceptable pain decreases.")
- **Short paragraphs.** Rarely more than 3-4 sentences.
- **Tables and visuals.** Loves using tables to compare, categorize, and create clarity. Almost every post has at least one.
- **Blockquotes for emphasis.** Pulls in quotes from Naval, Buffett, etc. — thinkers, not influencers.
- **Analogies and metaphors.** Uses vivid, unexpected comparisons. ("Like designing a new flavor of ice cream over email" / "Like building a sandcastle too close to the tide")
- **Ends with a punchy conclusion.** Short, decisive. Often a call to reflection, not action.

## Content Patterns
- **First-principles thinking.** Breaks complex topics into fundamental units, then rebuilds understanding.
- **Historical context.** Pulls from centuries of examples, not just tech trends.
- **Personal experience woven in.** Not memoir-style — quick anecdotes that serve the argument.
- **Contrarian takes.** Willing to say remote work hurts most companies, or that SF's tolerance creates problems. Doesn't chase popular opinion.
- **Cross-domain thinking.** Connects partnerships to co-founders, aunties to regulation, decision-making to OkCupid. Sees patterns across fields.

## What to Avoid
- Listicles or "10 tips" format — posts are essays with a thesis
- Filler sentences ("In this blog post, we will explore...")
- Excessive caveats or disclaimers
- Marketing language or hype
- Long intros before the point — get to it fast

## Anti-AI-Slop Rules

AI-assisted drafts must be scrubbed of these patterns before publishing. Readers will clock them instantly.

### Banned Words & Phrases
These are AI fingerprints. Never use them unironically:
- **Verbs:** delve, unpack, navigate, leverage, foster, harness, elevate, revolutionize, unleash, embark, underscore, bolster
- **Adjectives:** cutting-edge, multifaceted, comprehensive, vibrant, bustling, meticulous, nuanced, pivotal, seamless
- **Nouns:** landscape, tapestry, realm, journey, game-changer, paradigm shift, deep dive, synergy, ecosystem (when not literal)
- **Transitions:** moreover, furthermore, it's worth noting, in today's fast-paced world, as [X] continues to evolve, at the end of the day, that said
- **Filler openers:** In the ever-evolving, When it comes to, It is advisable, Let's explore

### Banned Structural Patterns
- **Snappy triads:** "Fast, efficient, and reliable." Three-beat cadences that sound punchy but say nothing.
- **"It's not just X — it's Y":** The formulaic reframe. ("It's not just a tool — it's a paradigm shift.") Kill on sight.
- **Unearned profundity:** "Something shifted." "But here's the thing." "Everything changed." These need to be *earned* by the content around them, not dropped as filler transitions.
- **Mid-sentence rhetorical questions:** "The solution? Simpler than you think." Feels like a TED talk on autopilot.
- **Em dash:** Never use em dash. Instead use normal "-". Don't over use it.
- **Quoted quotes:** Don't wrap blockquotes in quotation marks. The blockquote formatting already signals it's a quote. Use a blank `>` line then `> — Author` (em dash) for attribution.
- **Emoji bullet points:** 🎵 🚀 💡 as list markers. Never in a blog post.
- **Parallelism for its own sake:** Repeating the same sentence structure 3+ times in a row for false rhythm.
- **Bookend summaries:** Restating the intro in the conclusion almost word-for-word. The conclusion should advance the argument, not echo it.

### What to Do Instead
- **Use plain, specific language.** "The agent couldn't access the CMS" beats "The agent was unable to seamlessly integrate with the content management ecosystem."
- **Let awkwardness stay.** Real writing has rough edges. Not every sentence needs to flow perfectly into the next.
- **Be concrete.** Names, numbers, specific tools, exact errors. Specificity is the antidote to slop.
- **Vary sentence length naturally.** AI defaults to medium-length sentences. Mix in short punches. And occasionally a longer one that earns its length.
- **Cut the last paragraph.** AI always adds a wrap-up that restates everything. If the piece works without the final paragraph, delete it.
